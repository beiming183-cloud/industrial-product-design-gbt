# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Be respectful, specific, evidence-oriented, and welcoming. Critique the design, rule, test, or claim—not the person. Make room for industrial designers, engineers, CAD developers, manufacturers, students, and reviewers with different levels of experience.

Examples of positive participation include:

- explaining tradeoffs and evidence boundaries;
- acknowledging uncertainty and correcting mistakes;
- sharing reproducible fixtures instead of authority by assertion;
- protecting private, licensed, and safety-critical information;
- helping contributors turn vague findings into actionable issues.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discriminatory language, personal attacks, deliberate disclosure of private information, plagiarism, knowingly unsafe instructions, fabricated validation evidence, and repeated bad-faith disruption.

## Scope and enforcement

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other project spaces. Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub profile contact channel. The maintainer may edit, reject, lock, or remove contributions and restrict participation when needed to protect the community.

Enforcement decisions should be proportional, documented where appropriate, and focused on maintaining a safe and productive project.
