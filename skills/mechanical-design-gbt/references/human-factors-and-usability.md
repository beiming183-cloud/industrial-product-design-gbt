# Human Factors, Ergonomics, and Usability Evidence

Read this file when people touch, see, carry, install, adjust, clean, maintain, approach, or work around the product.

## Human-Centered Contract

Define before detailed geometry:

- intended and foreseeable users, including capability variation and accessibility needs;
- task, sequence, frequency, duration, urgency, consequence, and recovery;
- physical, social, lighting, noise, thermal, clothing, PPE, and space environment;
- body dimensions, reach, clearance, strength, vision, hearing, dexterity, and cognition that drive design;
- training, expertise, handedness, language, and foreseeable misuse;
- acceptance criteria and who may approve them.

Use ISO 9241-210 as a process-routing source for human-centred design when applicable. Use NASA HIDH and related public handbooks as method and background references only after checking population and environment transferability.

## Anthropometric Evidence

- Select a target population and current dataset; record sample, country or region, age, sex or gender basis, occupation, measurement definition, clothing or PPE, posture, date, and units.
- Do not design for a fictional average person. A 50th-percentile value does not represent a person who is 50th percentile in every dimension.
- Distinguish clearance design, reach design, strength or actuation design, adjustability, and accommodation range. Their limiting users may be different.
- Do not automatically combine percentiles from separate datasets or assume independence between body dimensions.
- Add allowances for motion, clothing, gloves, cables, tools, posture, and uncertainty only from a stated basis.
- Keep absent data `TBD`; use a sensitivity envelope for concept work rather than false precision.

## Task and Error Analysis

For setup, normal use, adjustment, cleaning, transport, service, and emergency action, record:

- trigger and user goal;
- ordered actions and decision points;
- reach, grip, posture, visibility, force, torque, and timing;
- system feedback and state visibility;
- likely slips, mistakes, confusion, obstruction, and misuse;
- error prevention, detection, recovery, and safe state;
- cable, hose, tool, consumable, and removed-part handling;
- evidence needed from mockup or test.

Review the complete task in every relevant product configuration. A control that works in the aligned state may be hidden, blocked, or unsafe in a rotated state.

## Control and Interface Review

- Make control-action and system-response direction compatible with the user's mental model and physical motion.
- Separate controls that are easily confused and distinguish critical controls by more than color alone where needed.
- Check labels, icons, contrast, viewing angle, glare, occlusion, status feedback, and low-light behavior.
- Test plugs and large adapters with neighboring interfaces, finger clearance, strain relief, and cable bends.
- Check one-handed, gloved, left-handed, weak-grip, seated, standing, and limited-reach cases when applicable.
- Treat force, comfort, readability, and reach as evidence questions, not visual impressions.

## Prototype and Test Ladder

Use the cheapest valid evidence in order:

1. task storyboard and reach or clearance envelopes;
2. full-scale paper, foam, or printed interface mockup;
3. adjustable ergonomic buck or weighted carry model;
4. functional interface and cable prototype;
5. integrated product prototype in representative context;
6. verification test with the defined target population and acceptance criteria.

Record protocol, participant or surrogate basis, product revision, configuration, environment, measures, failures, observations, and design changes. A positive informal comment is not a usability pass.

## Review Status

Use:

- `PASS`: defined users and tasks meet stated criteria with applicable evidence.
- `FAIL`: measured or observed evidence violates a requirement.
- `WARNING`: nonblocking risk with a named follow-up.
- `NOT_EVALUATED`: population, data, prototype, participant, environment, or test is absent.
- `ERROR`: the test or evidence chain is unreliable.

Never turn missing user evidence into a low-confidence pass.
